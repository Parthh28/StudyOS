StudyOS Indie Foundry Typography — Student Energy Edition

Philosophy: Stop using Google Fonts. Use fonts from actual designers who built type because they care, not because a corporation commissioned it.


The Indie Foundries — My Top Picks

🔥 #1 Recommended: Sequel 100 by OHno Type Co.

Why this screams "built by students":


Bold, geometric, unapologetically playful
Zero corporate energy
Used by design studios and forward-thinking startups, not every SaaS on the internet
Personality without being pretentious
Scans as "these people actually know type"


Best for: Headings, buttons, bold moments, "Start session" CTAs

Weights: Multiple weights available (100-900)

Pricing: ~$90 per weight (or variable font for flexibility)

License: Standard web license included

Where to buy: OHno Type Co. store

Why students love it: It's the font choice of apps that feel "designed by people who get it," not corporate consultants.


Maax by Grilli Type

Why this works for students:


Swiss modernism meets Gen-Z playfulness
Warm but not soft
Professional enough for exams, playful enough for motivation
Used by design-forward companies (not mainstream)
Has personality in every letter


Best for: Display font, primary headings, or paired with a humanist body

Weights: Variable font (7+ weights)

Pricing: ~$180-240 depending on license

License: Desktop + web included

Where to buy: Grilli Type store

Why it matters: Grilli Type is THE indie foundry for people who care. Every font is considered, not templated.


Untitled Sans by Klim Type Foundry

Why this is better for body text:


Distinctly warm and contemporary (not cold like most sans-serifs)
Designed to be readable for hours without fatigue
Used by apps that feel "designed by real humans"
Subtle personality — elegant without arrogance
Perfect for 4-6 hour study sessions


Best for: Body text, long copy, UI labels, supporting text

Weights: Variable font (full range)

Pricing: ~$130-200 depending on license

License: Desktop + web

Where to buy: Klim Type Foundry store

Why students prefer it: Doesn't feel corporate. Feels like someone actually wanted to make reading pleasant.


Clash Grotesk by Fontfabric

Why for bold students:


Bold geometric with attitude
Contemporary without being trendy
Energetic but maintains professionalism
MUCH more affordable than other options
Great range of weights


Best for: Primary headings, achievement badges, bold CTAs

Weights: Light to Bold

Pricing: ~$50 one weight, $150+ full family

License: Web license available

Where to buy: Fontfabric

Why it works: If you want boldness without the indie foundry premium price, this is the move.


My Top 3 Combos (Indie Only)

Combo 1: Sequel 100 + Untitled Sans (My Pick)

Headings:  Sequel 100 Bold (geometric, playful, bold)
Body:      Untitled Sans Regular (warm, readable, contemporary)
Data:      Space Mono (clean, technical)

Why: Sequel 100 screams "student-designed" in headings. Untitled Sans keeps body text warm and readable. No corporate vibes.
